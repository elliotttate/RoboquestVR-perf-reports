Technical-art evidence ledger

MED CONFIDENCESubmission workload is a measured GPU prioritization signal

Draw calls correlate +0.594 with app GPU time and -0.608 with FPS; submitted indices correlate +0.563 with GPU time across 24 matched replays. These moderate correlations rank controlled tests but do not prove a draw or mesh caused the cost.

HIGH CONFIDENCENo active-GameThread bottleneck appears in this run

The highest active GameThread sample is City B at 5.67 ms, while the worst GPU sample is BuggedCity B at 14.82 ms. The measured misses should be treated as GPU/submission or synchronization investigations, not CPU-only misses.

HIGH CONFIDENCETexture allocation is large but does not explain spot-to-spot frame cost

Captured texture allocation is 212.3–291.3 MiB, yet correlation with GPU time is only +0.074. Treat residency as a memory target, not the primary frame-time ranking.

MED CONFIDENCERHI occupancy is context, not an independent diagnosis

Median RHIThread occupancy is 39.7% of one core. Scheduler occupancy can help validate submission changes, but this percentage alone cannot identify which meshes, materials, or passes caused the work.

HIGH CONFIDENCEBatching efficiency is content dependent

The median capture submits 649 triangles per draw; the bottom captures fall below 350 triangles/draw. Median draws are 540, and the 90th percentile is 955.

HIGH CONFIDENCEThe observed compositor staleness is outcome evidence, not a root cause

Worst steady result is Fields A at 58.6 FPS and 15.8 stale frames/s. Stale rate nearly mirrors the 72 Hz deficit, so optimize the underlying CPU/GPU workload rather than the counter itself.

What the evidence does—and does not—prove

  • Per-level images are SceneColor exports taken immediately before the final large quest-card UI writes so the world is readable. RenderDoc counts and timing-linked metrics still represent the complete captured frame, including that overlay; do not subtract it without a controlled no-overlay re-run.
  • RenderDoc resource totals describe allocations present in the captured frame, not a proof that every resource contributed bandwidth to every draw.
  • The Adreno GPU-stage profiler returned valid 1680×1760, 2× MSAA eye-surface timing for 22/24 viewpoints; missing: BuggedCity A, BuggedCity B. These stage totals measure binning, render, store, and residual execution but do not identify per-draw shader ALU, texture bandwidth, overdraw, or occupancy.
  • Draw calls and submitted triangles are strongly correlated with GPU time, but correlation does not identify the specific mesh, material or shader without a controlled content edit and re-capture.
  • Perfetto thread occupancy is scheduler time on a core, not inclusive Unreal scope time; use the linked Insights traces for timer attribution.
  • The deterministic benchmark disables enemy AI and fixes the camera. It isolates scene/render cost well, but does not represent combat load or player-driven visibility changes.
